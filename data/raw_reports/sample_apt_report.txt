APT Campaign Report: ExampleRAT Targets Financial Sector

Published: 2025-10-01
Source: vendor_blog

Executive Summary

In Q3 2025, our research team observed a sustained campaign delivering the
ExampleRAT malware against financial institutions in North America and Europe.
The campaign is associated with FIN7 based on tooling and infrastructure overlap.

Initial Access

Victims received spearphishing emails containing weaponized documents. Upon
opening the attachment, the document spawned a PowerShell child process with
encoded command-line arguments. The encoded PowerShell command decoded a
base64 blob and downloaded a second-stage payload.

Execution and Persistence

ExampleRAT leverages PowerShell to execute its in-memory payload. The dropper
uses certutil.exe to transfer additional tools from the attacker C2 server at
example-c2.com. We also observed rundll32.exe being abused to proxy execution
of a malicious DLL.

Command and Control

The malware beacons to example-c2.com over HTTPS and falls back to the IP
198.51.100.23 when the primary C2 is unavailable. Sample SHA256:
abcdef0123456789abcdef0123456789abcdef0123456789abcdef0123456789.

Impact

Once inside, FIN7 operators were observed using Mimikatz to dump credentials
from LSASS and pivot to additional hosts via WMI (wmic process call create).

References

This activity overlaps with prior FIN7 reporting. Mapping to MITRE ATT&CK:
T1059.001 PowerShell, T1105 Ingress Tool Transfer, T1218.011 Rundll32,
T1003 OS Credential Dumping, T1047 Windows Management Instrumentation.
